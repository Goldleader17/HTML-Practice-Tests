You have a unique financial fingerprint that is formed by patterns in your:

  • Bank accounts
  • Income
  • Net worth 

 

At any one point in time, your financial fingerprint tends to bounce between a 'floor' and a 'ceiling'.

You might call these levels your 'lower financial thermostat' and your 'upper financial thermostat'.

These are called 'thermostats' because as you perceive (via sensory input) that your financial situation is approaching these levels, your actions (via motor output) are automatically (often unconsciously) adjusted to keep you in 'the middle'.

 

Another way of describing 'the middle' is:

  • Your comfort zone 
  • Your overall financial thermostat
  • Your internal financial level
  • Your financial self-image
  • Your financial self-worth

 

As your financial situation approaches or exceeds your upper financial thermostat:

  •  You start loosening up
  • You become more financially sloppy
  • You take greater risks
  • Your work ethic & consistency declines 
  • Your lifestyle & expenses increase
  • You attract people, situations & circumstances to have your money 'taken'
  • You manifest unexpected bills or financial emergencies 

 

As your financial situation approaches or exceeds your lower financial thermostat:

  •  You start tightening up 
  • You become more financially calculated
  • You take fewer risks
  • Your work ethic & consistency increases 
  • Your lifestyle & expenses decrease
  • You attract people, situations & circumstances to provide new income opportunities 
  • You manifest unexpected income or financial wins

 

Out of the 2 thermostats, your lower financial thermostat is by far the most significant contributor to your overall financial situation. 

Your upper financial thermostat could be likened to 'dreams'.

Your lower financial thermostat could be likened to 'standards'.

You don't always achieve your dreams, but you'll always uphold your standards.

To increase your overall financial situation, you must work on increasing your standards.

 

Based on my experience, these are the 6 primary components that govern your standards:

  1. Beliefs -> What do you believe about money, wealth, rich people, and poor people? 
  2. Values -> Where does wealth sit on your hierarchy of values?
  3. Influence -> Who do you hang around most, and what is their financial situation?
  4. Language -> How do you speak about money, wealth, income, finance, and affordability? 
  5. Habits -> What are your habits around making and spending money? 
  6. Worthiness -> At the deepest level of your being, how much money do you truly believe you deserve?

 

Ask yourself: 

Who do I have to become to be where I want to be? 

You have to become the person to achieve the goals that are associated with that person. 

You are not the highest version of yourself that you can imagine/dream about, you are the lowest person which you can possibly accept and tolerate. 

You're not you're highest dreams, you're your lowest standards. 

You can dream as high as you want, but you are not going to achieve it with your current character. 

How you achieve your dreams is to transcend your current self, and turn into a character who has your dreams as its standards.

The hack designs a character who has their dreams as your standards -> become that character and you achieve those dreams.
