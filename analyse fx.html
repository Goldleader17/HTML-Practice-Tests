Market Mechanics

Currency exchange rates move up and down as a result of supply and demand from market participants. 

Every day, investors and traders, both institutional and private individuals, all buy and sell currencies. 

The numerous types of market participants are buying or selling for their own unique reasons, with their own unique views, ideas, and beliefs around what the currency pairs they are trading are worth. 

The meeting point of buyers and sellers, for whatever reason they are acting, is price. 

No trades can take place unless both the buyer and the seller agree on a price. 

So this drives the price of currency pairs buyers bring with them demand for the pair, applying upward pressure on prices, while sellers bring supply, applying downward pressure on prices. 

The market runs like a continuous auction throughout the day, with buyers and sellers competing with each other to get the best possible price.

 

Technical Analysis -> the framework used to identify trading opportunities through the study of price movement and volume from that battle between buyers and sellers, which is then reflected on a price chart.

Supply & Demand drives -> Orderflow which prints -> Price Action that creates -> Patterns

The interaction of millions of different participants for as many reasons, all with their own emotions and agendas, forms price patterns that stretch over extended periods of time and can be used to make high probability forecasts of where price may move in the future. 

Candlesticks -> visual representation of price fluctuations in the market for a set timeframe.
