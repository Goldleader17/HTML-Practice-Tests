The number one reason traders fail is due to an absence of any risk management plus the abuse of leverage. 

You are a professional risk manager. 

Protection of capital > optimisation of profit.

Potential profits are seductive. 

 

Golden Rule -> Never risk more than 1% of your trading account per trade.  

Reverse compounding forces your risk to get smaller during inevitable periods of drawdown, which will fix your maximum downside, ensuring it's then mathematically impossible to blow your account. 

The Golden Rule eliminates the risk that is you. 

 

Drawdown -> reduction of capital after a series of losing trades. 

Measured by a relative peak in capital minus a relative trough in capital. 

Recovering a loss is mathematically harder than making the loss in the first place. 

 

 

 

 

All of your trades should end in one of four ways:

  1. A small win
  2. A big win
  3. A small loss
  4. Breakeven

Eliminating large losses will keep you in the game for years to come.

If you can't risk manage on a 'small' account, you won't be able to do it on a large account.
